$ sw optimize_click_freeze

 1. In the following steps, setup these conditions:

    ligation/RT arms (o233):  +  −   +    +    +    +    +    +
    puromycin arm (o126):     −  +   +    +    +    +    +    +
    PBS:                      −  −   +    +    +    −    −    −
    25°C incubation:          −  −   −   4h   o/n   −   4h   o/n
    -20°C incubation:         −  −  o/n  o/n   −   o/n  o/n   −

 2. Setup 2 click reactions:

    Reagent   Stock   Volume       2x
    ─────────────────────────────────
    PBS          2x  1.50 µL
    o126     400 µM  0.75 µL  1.50 µL
    o233     400 µM  0.75 µL  1.50 µL
    ─────────────────────────────────
                     3.00 µL  1.50 µL/rxn

 3. Divide each reaction in three.

 4. Incubate the reactions as follows:

    - −20°C overnight
    - 25°C for 4h, −20°C overnight
    - 25°C overnight

 5. Label the product: o236

 6. Prepare 8 samples for TBE/urea PAGE:

    Reagent             Stock    Volume       12x
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────
    water                       4.88 µL  58.61 µL
    sample buffer          2x   5.00 µL  60.00 µL
    sample         1730 ng/µL   0.12 µL
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────
                               10.00 µL   9.88 µL/rxn

    - Incubate at 70°C for 3 min.

 7. Run a gel [1]:

    gel: 6% TBE/urea PAGE
    buffer: 1x TBE
    samples: 10 µL
    run: 180V for 40 min

 8. Stain gel with SYBR Green II [2,3]:

    - Keep the stain protected from light.

    - Submerge gel in ≈30 mL 1x SYBR Green II, 1x TBE
      (89 mM Tris, 89 mM boric acid, 1 mM EDTA,
      pH=8.0).
    - Shake gently for 10m.

 9. Image with a 300 nm UV transilluminator.

10. Image with a laser scanner:

    lasers:   488 nm   658 nm
    filters:  518BP22  710BP40

Notes:
[1] The product (o236) has MW = 17285 g/mol, so:
    100 µM = 1730 ng/µL

[2] https://tinyurl.com/y8n7237s

[3] Staining with SYBR Green II reagent is pH
    sensitive.  For optimal sensitivity, verify that
    the pH of the staining solution at the
    temperature used for staining is between 7.5 and
    8.0 (preferably 8.0).
