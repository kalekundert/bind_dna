1. Setup 1 HaeII digestion [1,2]:

   Reagent               Stock    Volume
   ─────────────────────────────────────
   water                         4.15 µL
   p2                230 ng/µL   4.35 µL
   rCutSmart Buffer        10x   1.00 µL
   HaeII               20 U/µL   0.50 µL
   ─────────────────────────────────────
                                10.00 µL

2. Incubate at the following temperatures [3]:

   - 37°C for 5–15 min
   - 80°C for 20 min

3. Setup 1 ligation reaction [4]:

   Reagent               Stock   Volume
   ────────────────────────────────────
   water                        3.27 µL
   T4 ligase buffer        10x  0.50 µL
   T4 DNA ligase      400 U/µL  0.25 µL
   HaeII fragments   100 ng/µL  0.98 µL
   ────────────────────────────────────
                                5.00 µL

4. Incubate at the following temperatures:

   - 25°C for 15 min
   - 65°C for 10 min

5. Run a 1% E-gel.

Notes:
[1] https://www.neb.com/products/r0107-haeii

[2] NEB recommends 5–10 units of enzyme per µg DNA
    (10–20 units for genomic DNA).  Enzyme volume
    should not exceed 10% of the total reaction
    volume to prevent star activity due to excess
    glycerol.

[3] The heat inactivation step is not necessary if
    the DNA will be purified before use.
