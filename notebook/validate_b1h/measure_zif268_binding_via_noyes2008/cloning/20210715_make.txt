$ sw make p168

 1. Setup 1 annealing reaction:

    Reagent   Stock   Volume
    ────────────────────────
    PBS         10x  0.40 µL
    o190     100 µM  1.80 µL
    o191     100 µM  1.80 µL
    ────────────────────────
                     4.00 µL

 2. Perform the annealing reaction:

    - Incubate at 95°C for 2 min.
    - Cool at room temperature.

 3. Label the product: f80

 4. Setup 1 restriction digestion [1,2]:

    Reagent              Stock    Volume
    ────────────────────────────────────
    p167              84 ng/µL  11.90 µL
    rCutSmart Buffer       10x   1.43 µL
    EcoRI-HF           20 U/µL   0.50 µL
    NotI-HF            20 U/µL   0.50 µL
    ────────────────────────────────────
                                14.34 µL

 5. Incubate at the following temperatures [3]:

    - 37°C for 5–15 min
    - 65°C for 20 min

 6. Label the product: f79

 7. Setup 1 ligation reaction [4]:

    Reagent               Stock   Volume
    ────────────────────────────────────
    water                        0.10 µL
    T4 ligase buffer        10x  0.50 µL
    T4 DNA ligase      400 U/µL  0.25 µL
    f79               100 ng/µL  2.15 µL
    f80                   90 nM  2.00 µL
    ────────────────────────────────────
                                 5.00 µL

 8. Incubate at the following temperatures:

    - 25°C for 15 min
    - 65°C for 10 min

 9. Label the product: p168

10. Miniprep.

Notes:
[1] https://www.neb.com/products/r3101-ecori-hf
    https://www.neb.com/products/r3189-noti-hf

[2] NEB recommends 5–10 units of enzyme per µg DNA
    (10–20 units for genomic DNA).  Enzyme volume
    should not exceed 10% of the total reaction
    volume to prevent star activity due to excess
    glycerol.

[3] The heat inactivation step is not necessary if
    the DNA will be purified before use.
