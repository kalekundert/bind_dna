$ sw make f195

1. Setup 1 BsmBI-v2 digestion [1,2]:

   Reagent            Stock     Volume
   ───────────────────────────────────
   water                     206.95 µL
   p237           383 ng/µL   13.05 µL
   NEBuffer r3.1        10x   25.00 µL
   BsmBI-v2         10 U/µL    5.00 µL
   ───────────────────────────────────
                             250.00 µL

2. Incubate at the following temperatures [3]:

   - 55°C for 2 h
   - 80°C for 20 min

3. Label the product: f195

4. Prepare 1 sample for electrophoresis:

   Reagent      Stock    Volume
   ────────────────────────────
   f195                50.00 µL
   loading dye     6x  10.00 µL
   ────────────────────────────
                       60.00 µL

5. Run a gel:

   gel: 1% agarose
   buffer: 0.5x tris-acetate
   ladder: 10 µL 50 ng/µL 1kb+ DNA ladder
   samples: 60 µL/lane
   run: 100V for 90 min

6. Stain gel with GelGreen [4-6]:

   - Keep the stain protected from light.

   - Submerge gel in ≈30 mL 1x GelGreen, 1x running
     buffer.
   - Shake gently for 30m.

7. Purify the desired band using the QIAEX II kit:

   - Cut the 2.4kb band out of the gel.

   - Add 3 volumes QX1.
   - Add 30 µL QIAEX II beads.
     - Resuspend the beads by vortexing for 30s.
   - Incubate at 50°C for 10 min.
     - Vortex every 2 min to keep the beads
       suspended.
     - If the solution is not yellow after the
       agarose dissolves, add 10 µL 3M sodium acetate
       pH=5 and continue incubating for at least 5
       min.
   - Centrifuge for 30s.  Discard supernatant.

   - Add 500 µL QX1.
   - Vortex to resuspend the pellet.
   - Centrifuge for 30s.  Discard supernatant.

   - Repeat 2x:
     - Add 500 µL PE.
     - Vortex to resuspend the pellet.
     - Centrifuge for 30s.  Discard supernatant.

   - Air-dry the pellet for 30 min.

   - Add 20 µL EB.
   - Incubate at room temperature for 5 min.
   - Centrifuge for 30s.  Transfer supernatant to a
     clean tube.

Notes:
[1] https://www.neb.com/products/r0739-bsmbi-v2

[2] NEB recommends 5–10 units of enzyme per µg DNA
    (10–20 units for genomic DNA).  Enzyme volume
    should not exceed 10% of the total reaction
    volume to prevent star activity due to excess
    glycerol.

[3] The heat inactivation step is not necessary if
    the DNA will be purified before use.

[4] https://tinyurl.com/etxhfxws

[5] Including 100 mM NaCl in the staining solution
    enhances sensitivity, but may promote dye
    precipitation if the gel stain is reused.

[6] Staining solution can be reused at least 2-3x.
    Store staining solution at room temperature
    protected from light.
