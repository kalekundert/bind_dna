$ sw make f83

 1. Prepare 10x primer mix [1]:

    Reagent   Stock    Volume
    ─────────────────────────
    water             9.00 µL
    o109     100 µM   0.50 µL
    o110     100 µM   0.50 µL
    ─────────────────────────
                     10.00 µL

 2. Setup 1 PCR reaction [2,3]:

    Reagent           Stock    Volume
    ─────────────────────────────────
    water                    15.00 µL
    f81            20 pg/µL   5.00 µL
    primer mix          10x   5.00 µL
    Q5 master mix        2x  25.00 µL
    ─────────────────────────────────
                             50.00 µL

 3. Run the following thermocycler protocol:

    - 98°C for 30s
    - Repeat 35x:
      - 98°C for 10s
      - 67°C for 20s
      - 72°C for 1 min
    - 72°C for 2 min
    - 4°C hold

 4. Label the product: f83

 5. Concentrate the sample to 10 uL by
    lyophilization.

 6. Prepare 1 sample for electrophoresis:

    Reagent         Stock   Volume
    ──────────────────────────────
    loading buffer     6x  1.00 µL
    f83                    5.00 µL
    ──────────────────────────────
                           6.00 µL

 7. Run a gel:

    gel: 1% agarose/TAE
    buffer: 1x TAE
    samples: 5 µL/lane
    run: 100V for 60 min

 8. Stain gel with GelGreen [4-6]:

    - Keep the stain protected from light.

    - Submerge gel in ≈30 mL 3x GelGreen, 100 mM
      NaCl.
    - Shake gently for 30m.

 9. Image with a laser scanner:

    laser:    488 nm
    filter:  518BP22

10. Cut desired band out of an agarose gel.

11. Place the gel slice in a Spin-X column with
    1 µL/mg EB.  Spin at 13,000g for 5-20 min
    at room temperature [7].
    
    The following optional steps may improve yield:
    
    - Macerate the gel.
    - Freeze/thaw the gel.
    - Add 200 µL EB after the first spin and
      re-centrifuge.
    - Spin for longer.

12. Purify using Qiagen MinElute reaction cleanup kit
    (28204) [8,9]:

    - Perform all spin steps at 17900g.
    - Ensure that the sample is between 20–100 µL.
    - Add 300 µL ERC to the crude DNA.
    - If not yellow: Add 10 µL 3M sodium acetate,
      pH=5.0.
    - Load on a MinElute column.
    - Spin 1m; discard flow-through.

    - Add 750 µL PE.
    - Spin 1m; discard flow-through.
    - Spin 1m; discard flow-through.
    - Add 10.0 µL EB.
    - Wait at least 1m.
    - Spin 1m; keep flow-through.

Notes:
[1] For resuspending lyophilized primers:
    100 µM = 10 µL/nmol

[2] https://tinyurl.com/y27ralt4

[3] For diluting template DNA to 20 pg/µL:
    Dilute 1 µL twice into 7*sqrt([DNA]) µL

[4] https://tinyurl.com/etxhfxws

[5] Including 100 mM NaCl in the staining solution
    enhances sensitivity, but may promote dye
    precipitation if the gel stain is reused.

[6] Staining solution can be reused at least 2-3x.
    Store staining solution at room temperature
    protected from light.

[7] https://tinyurl.com/fh2jtd
    
    The official protocol just recommends 100-200 µL 
    EB, but I personally find that 1 µL/mg is a more 
    robust approach.

[8] https://www.qiagen.com/HB-2069

[9] Column capacity: 5 µg
