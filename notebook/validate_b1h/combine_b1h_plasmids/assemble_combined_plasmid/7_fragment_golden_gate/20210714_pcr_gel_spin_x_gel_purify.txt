1. Prepare 10x primer mix [1]:

   Reagent   Stock    Volume
   ─────────────────────────
   water             9.00 µL
   o244     100 µM   0.50 µL
   o245     100 µM   0.50 µL
   ─────────────────────────
                    10.00 µL

2. Setup 1 PCR reaction [2,3]:

   Reagent           Stock    Volume
   ─────────────────────────────────
   water                    15.00 µL
   p167           20 pg/µL   5.00 µL
   primer mix          10x   5.00 µL
   Q5 master mix        2x  25.00 µL
   ─────────────────────────────────
                            50.00 µL

3. Run the following thermocycler protocol:

   - 98°C for 30s
   - Repeat 35x:
     - 98°C for 10s
     - 59°C for 20s
     - 72°C for 3 min
   - 72°C for 2 min
   - 4°C hold

4. Prepare 1 sample for electrophoresis:

   Reagent         Stock    Volume
   ───────────────────────────────
   loading buffer     6x  10.00 µL
   sample                 50.00 µL
   ───────────────────────────────
                          60.00 µL

5. Run a gel:

   gel: 1% agarose/TAE with 1x GelGreen
   buffer: 1x TAE
   samples: 30 µL/lane
   run: 100V for 60 min

6. Image with a 470 nm blue-light transilluminator.

7. Cut desired band out of an agarose gel.

8. Place the gel slice in a Spin-X column with
   100-200 µL EB.  Spin at 13,000g for 5-20 min
   at room temperature [4].
   
   The following optional steps may improve yield:
   
   - Macerate the gel.
   - Freeze/thaw the gel.
   - Add 200 µL EB after the first spin and
     re-centrifuge.
   - Spin for longer.

Notes:
[1] For resuspending lyophilized primers:
    100 µM = 10 µL/nmol

[2] https://tinyurl.com/y27ralt4

[3] For diluting template DNA to 20 pg/µL:
    Dilute 1 µL twice into 7*sqrt([DNA]) µL
