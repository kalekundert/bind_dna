$ sw pcr -u f83 -v5 -g14 -a65 -n10
$ sw gels/gel agarose/1 10 -r40

1. Prepare 10x primer mix [1]:

   Reagent   Stock    Volume
   ─────────────────────────
   water             9.00 µL
   o109     100 µM   0.50 µL
   o110     100 µM   0.50 µL
   ─────────────────────────
                    10.00 µL

2. Setup 10 PCR reactions [2,3]:

   Reagent           Stock   Volume       12x
   ──────────────────────────────────────────
   water                    1.50 µL  18.00 µL
   f81            20 pg/µL  0.50 µL   6.00 µL
   primer mix          10x  0.50 µL   6.00 µL
   Q5 master mix        2x  2.50 µL  30.00 µL
   ──────────────────────────────────────────
                            5.00 µL   5.00 µL/rxn

   - Use any extra master mix as a negative control.

3. Run the following thermocycler protocol:

   - 98°C for 30s
   - Repeat 35x:
     - 98°C for 10s
     - 58-72°C for 20s
     - 72°C for 1 min
   - 72°C for 2 min
   - 4°C hold

4. Prepare 10 samples for electrophoresis:

   Reagent         Stock   Volume
   ──────────────────────────────
   loading buffer     6x  1.00 µL
   sample                 5.00 µL
   ──────────────────────────────
                          6.00 µL

5. Run a gel:

   gel: 1% agarose/TAE
   buffer: 1x TAE
   samples: 5 µL/lane
   run: 100V for 40 min

6. Stain gel with GelGreen [4-6]:

   - Keep the stain protected from light.

   - Submerge gel in ≈30 mL 3x GelGreen, 100 mM NaCl.
   - Shake gently for 30m.

7. Image with a laser scanner:

   laser:    488 nm
   filter:  518BP22

Notes:
[1] For resuspending lyophilized primers:
    100 µM = 10 µL/nmol

[2] https://tinyurl.com/y27ralt4

[3] For diluting template DNA to 20 pg/µL:
    Dilute 1 µL twice into 7*sqrt([DNA]) µL

[4] https://tinyurl.com/etxhfxws

[5] Including 100 mM NaCl in the staining solution
    enhances sensitivity, but may promote dye
    precipitation if the gel stain is reused.

[6] Staining solution can be reused at least 2-3x.
    Store staining solution at room temperature
    protected from light.
