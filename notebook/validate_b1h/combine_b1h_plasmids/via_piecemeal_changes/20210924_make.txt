$ sw make p184 p185 p186

 1. Prepare 10x primer mix [1]:

    Reagent          Stock    Volume
    ────────────────────────────────
    water                    9.00 µL
    o256,o258,o260  100 µM   0.50 µL
    o257,o259,o261  100 µM   0.50 µL
    ────────────────────────────────
                            10.00 µL

 2. Setup 3 PCR reactions [2,3]:

    Reagent           Stock    Volume      3.3x
    ───────────────────────────────────────────
    water                    15.00 µL  49.50 µL
    p183           20 pg/µL   5.00 µL  16.50 µL
    primer mix          10x   5.00 µL
    Q5 master mix        2x  25.00 µL  82.50 µL
    ───────────────────────────────────────────
                             50.00 µL  45.00 µL/rxn

    - Use any extra master mix as a negative control.

 3. Run the following thermocycler protocol:

    - 98°C for 30s
    - Repeat 35x:
      - 98°C for 10s
      - 64°C,65°C,66°C for 20s
      - 72°C for 3 min
    - 72°C for 2 min
    - 4°C hold

 4. Label the products: f136, f137, f138

 5. Prepare 3 samples for electrophoresis:

    Reagent         Stock   Volume
    ──────────────────────────────
    loading buffer     6x  1.00 µL
    sample                 5.00 µL
    ──────────────────────────────
                           6.00 µL

 6. Run a gel:

    gel: 1% agarose/TBE with 1x GelGreen
    buffer: 1x TBE
    samples: 5 µL/lane
    run: 100V for 60 min

 7. Image with a 470 nm blue-light transilluminator.

 8. Cut desired band out of an agarose gel.

 9. Place the gel slice in a Spin-X column with
    1 µL/mg EB.  Spin at 13,000g for 5-20 min
    at room temperature [4].
    
    The following optional steps may improve yield:
    
    - Macerate the gel.
    - Freeze/thaw the gel.
    - Add 200 µL EB after the first spin and
      re-centrifuge.
    - Spin for longer.

10. Purify using Qiagen MinElute PCR purification kit
    (28004) [5,6]:

    - Perform all spin steps at 17900g.
    - Add 5 volumes PB to the crude DNA.
    - If not yellow: Add 0.2 volumes 3M sodium
      acetate, pH=5.0.
    - Load on a MinElute column.
    - Spin 1m; discard flow-through.

    - Add 750 µL PE.
    - Spin 1m; discard flow-through.
    - Spin 1m; discard flow-through.
    - Add 10.0 µL EB.
    - Wait at least 1m.
    - Spin 1m; keep flow-through.

11. Setup 3 Golden Gate assemblies [7]:

    Reagent               Stock   Volume       4x
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────
    water                        2.17 µL  8.68 µL
    T4 ligase buffer        10x  0.50 µL  2.00 µL
    T4 DNA ligase      400 U/µL  0.12 µL  0.50 µL
    BsaI-HFv2           20 U/µL  0.12 µL  0.50 µL
    f136,f137,f138    200 ng/µL  1.10 µL
    f38                80 ng/uL  0.24 µL  0.94 µL
    f82               124 ng/uL  0.56 µL  2.26 µL
    f36                67 ng/uL  0.18 µL  0.73 µL
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────
                                 5.00 µL  3.90 µL/rxn

12. Run the following thermocycler protocol:

    - 37°C for 60 min
    - 60°C for 5 min

13. Label the products: p184, p185, p186

14. Miniprep.

Notes:

    100 µM = 10 µL/nmol

    Dilute 1 µL twice into 7*sqrt([DNA]) µL

    The official protocol just recommends 100-200 µL 
    EB, but I personally find that 1 µL/mg is a more 
    robust approach.
